01
The Business Problem — Emergency Departments Are in Crisis
⏱️
Clinicians Spend More Time Searching Than Treating
Studies show emergency physicians spend up to 35% of their shift navigating fragmented EHR systems — 6–10 minutes per patient locating allergy records, medication lists, and prior visit history buried across multiple screens.
6–10 min / patient
⚠️
Information Gaps Cause Preventable Harm
Medication errors affect 1.3 million people annually in the US. Missed allergy flags cause thousands of preventable adverse reactions. Every 10-minute delay in sepsis care increases patient mortality risk by 7%.
1.3M medication errors / yr
🔥
Staff Burnout Is at Breaking Point
62% of emergency physicians report burnout — driven by administrative and cognitive burden, not clinical work. Turnover costs $40,000–$60,000 per ED nurse. The US faces a projected shortage of 124,000 physicians by 2034.
$40K–$60K turnover cost
🏥
EDs Are Operating Over Capacity
145 million annual US ED visits. Average wait of 2h 15m before seeing a physician. 1 in 5 patients leaves without being seen. ED crowding is directly correlated with increased patient mortality.
1 in 5 patients leave LWBS
🗂️
Healthcare Data Is Fragmented
Hospitals operate with patient data siloed across EHR systems, pharmacy, labs, radiology PACS, and legacy ADT platforms. Staff context-switch between 5–10 systems per patient interaction. The right information exists but cannot be reached fast enough.
5–10 systems per patient
💸
Financial Impact Is Significant
Adverse drug events cost an average $4,700 per incident. LWBS patients represent $1,500+ in lost revenue per visit. Malpractice exposure, agency staffing costs, and CMS quality score penalties compound the financial drain.
$4,700 avg per adverse event

The Solution — What the ED AI Agent Does

Natural-Language Clinical Queries

9 MCP Database Tools
Staff Query Agent Action Time Saved
"Show me all critical patients right now" Queries ESI 1–2 triage records with vitals and known allergies ~8 min → 10 sec
"What medications is the patient on?" Fetches complete medication list with dosage, route, frequency ~5 min → 8 sec
"Who is allergic to penicillin today?" Cross-references allergy records against today's arrivals ~10 min → 12 sec
"What were the patient's vitals on arrival?" Pulls triage record and first vital sign set by MRN ~4 min → 7 sec
"How many arrived by ambulance this week?" Runs aggregation query across triage records ~15 min → 6 sec
"Register a new patient — chest pain, ESI 2" Writes a new patient intake record to the database ~6 min → 45 sec

Patient Safety Improvements

🛡️
Allergy warnings surface automatically — the agent proactively flags contraindications every time a patient with known allergies is queried
📊
Abnormal vital thresholds are hard-coded (HR > 100, SBP < 90, SpO₂ < 95%) and flagged in every patient summary
🔴
ESI 1–2 patients always highlighted as critical, reducing risk of high-acuity patients being overlooked in a crowded department
📋
Code status (Full Code vs. DNR) is surfaced with every patient record, preventing inappropriate resuscitation

AI Capability

2024–2026 marks the first practical window in which large language models are sufficiently capable and cost-effective to deploy clinically. AI inference costs have dropped 100× in three years — deploying an intelligent agent per query now costs cents, not dollars.
100× cost reduction

Protocol Standard
Model Context Protocol Changes Integration
MCP (2024) provides a standardized, secure interface between AI models and any database or data source — making integrations achievable in days instead of months. The same protocol will connect to Epic FHIR, labs, and real-time monitors with zero changes to the agent.
Days not months

CMS star ratings and HCAHPS scores now drive reimbursement. Hospitals measured on readmission rates, patient safety indicators, and quality metrics — all directly improved by AI decision support. The FDA has established a clear regulatory pathway for clinical decision support software.
Value-based care mandate
